## Competitive Moat & Positioning

### Why Bastelschachtel Wins (Unfair Advantages)

1. **Multi-specialist positioning** — Own Pentart + Reispapier + Korbflechten + Molds equally. Competitors are single-specialty or generic.

2. **Curation over scale** — Deliberate product selection for projects that work. Reduces decision paralysis.

3. **Personal, non-corporate tone** — Fundamental to brand. Comes through in copy, packaging, service. Cannot be replicated at scale.

4. **Permission-based positioning** — Recognize existing desire, don't create fake urgency. Most competitors use scarcity/FOMO.

5. **Small-team authenticity** — 4-person team in Wattens, hand-packing, direct phone line. Actual, not simulated.

6. **Austrian/local origin** — Genuine regional authenticity (Tirol). Competitors hide origin; Bastelschachtel leads with it.

### What Competitors Get Right (Match This)
- Broad product selection (convenience)
- Fast logistics
- Easy returns
- SEO basics (structure, mobile, speed)

### What NOT to Compete On
- Price per unit (Amazon scales better)
- Selection breadth in commodities
- Fastest shipping
- Lowest-friction checkout

## German Language Standards

All content follows:
- **Capitalization:** All nouns capitalized (Bastelschachtel, Material, Projekt, etc.)
- **Formality:** Use "du" (you, singular) not formal "Sie"
- **Active voice:** 80%+ active for conversational tone
- **Paragraph length:** 2-4 sentences max
- **Sentence length:** 15-20 words average
- **Punctuation:** German comma rules (more commas than English)
