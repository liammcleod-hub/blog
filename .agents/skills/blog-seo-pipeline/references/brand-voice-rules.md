## Core Rule

The article should sound like Bastelschachtel, not like a content system, SEO script, or conversion page.

## Desired Voice

- warm
- personal
- calm
- helpful
- lightly guided, not pushy
- product-present but not catalog-heavy

## Voice Failure Modes

Flag or rewrite:

- pipeline-revealing phrases such as `in deinem Setup` or `in deinem Produktkontext`
- tool-like phrasing
- catalog-like repetition
- overly transactional product pushes
- corporate or formal register
- urgency language not grounded in fact
- ⭐ **NEW:** Composed/literary language that sounds like you're performing warmth
- ⭐ **NEW:** Prescriptive language ("genau richtig", "perfekt")
- ⭐ **NEW:** Explicit permission statements ("Das ist vollkommen in Ordnung" repeated)
- ⭐ **NEW:** Over-stacked emphasis (three+ emphasis words in one sentence)

## Practical Rule

For blog content, the reader should feel:

- guided
- understood
- less intimidated

not:

- sold to
- processed
- optimized against

## Rewrite Direction

When a sentence feels too system-generated, prefer:

- concrete beginner framing
- sensory or practical specificity
- plain spoken `du` phrasing
- softer transitions
- **conversational rhythm over composed prose** ⭐
- **warm colloquialisms** ("überhaupt kein Problem", "bei uns direkt") ⭐
- **permission shown through tone, not stated** ⭐

Instead of:

- `In deinem aktuellen Produktkontext ist 2mm...`

Prefer:

- `Fuer den Einstieg ist 2mm oft die entspanntere Wahl...`

Instead of:

- `Das ist vollkommen in Ordnung. Du musst das nicht machen.` ⭐

Prefer:

- `Wenn dir das zu viel ist, überhaupt kein Problem. Wir haben hier auch...` ⭐
