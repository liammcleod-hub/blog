# Brand Voice Rules

This reference tells `blog-seo-pipeline` how to apply Bastelschachtel brand voice during article QA and revision.

## Core Rule

The article should sound like Bastelschachtel, not like a content system, SEO script, or conversion page.

## Desired Voice

- warm
- personal
- calm
- helpful
- lightly guided, not pushy
- product-present but not catalog-heavy

## Voice Failure Modes

Flag or rewrite:

- pipeline-revealing phrases such as `in deinem Setup` or `in deinem Produktkontext`
- tool-like phrasing
- catalog-like repetition
- overly transactional product pushes
- corporate or formal register
- urgency language not grounded in fact
- research-exhaust sections that feel like leftover source comparison instead of customer guidance
- visible technical labels such as `Quellen` when they make the article feel academic or machine-assembled without helping the reader

## Practical Rule

For blog content, the reader should feel:

- guided
- understood
- less intimidated

not:

- sold to
- processed
- optimized against

## Rewrite Direction

When a sentence feels too system-generated, prefer:

- concrete beginner framing
- sensory or practical specificity
- plain spoken `du` phrasing
- softer transitions

Instead of:

- `In deinem aktuellen Produktkontext ist 2mm...`

Prefer:

- `Fuer den Einstieg ist 2mm oft die entspanntere Wahl...`

## Bastelschachtel Test

If the article is working, it should feel like a knowledgeable person from Bastelschachtel is helping a beginner start calmly.

The reader should not feel:

- processed
- summarized at
- moved through a research artifact

Prefer:

- calm reassurance
- practical next steps
- small-shop helpfulness
- concrete project guidance over abstract authority language
