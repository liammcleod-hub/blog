# SEO Inference Rules

This reference defines how `blog-seo-pipeline` should infer required article structure beyond what is explicitly stated in the brief.

## Core Rule

The final article must make sense according to:

1. the live research dossier
2. the Bastelschachtel SEO audit and gap map
3. the relevant mirrored SEO skills

Do not evaluate the article only against the brief.

## Required Inputs

When available, read:

- `docs/seo/bastelschachtel_seo_audit.docx`
- `repo-skills/marketing-library/skills/seo-audit/SKILL.md`
- `repo-skills/marketing-library/skills/programmatic-seo/SKILL.md`
- `repo-skills/marketing-library/skills/ai-seo/SKILL.md`

## What to Infer

Infer:

- what type of content is actually winning in search
- what content gaps the Bastelschachtel site needs to close
- what supporting sections are needed for search-intent coverage
- what makes the page non-thin and genuinely useful

## Practical Inference Questions

For any job, ask:

- Is this topic mainly tutorial-driven, comparison-driven, inspirational, commercial, or mixed?
- Does the audit imply Bastelschachtel needs more informational blog content for this query class?
- Does the article include the supporting blocks required to satisfy the mixed intent?
- Does the page provide enough unique value per section to avoid thin-content behavior?
- Is the content extractable enough for AI search and answer engines?

## Typical Required Blocks

Depending on the topic, the inferred structure may require some of:

- direct-answer intro block
- definition block
- methodology or ranking-criteria block
- step-by-step preparation block
- maintenance or care section
- comparison block
- FAQ block
- internal-link support block
- source-backed table

## Example: Peddigrohr

For `peddigrohr`, the dossier and search landscape show a mix of:

- crafting/how-to intent
- maintenance intent
- comparison intent
- inspiration intent

So even if the brief is a listicle, the page may still need:

- a short `what is peddigrohr` explanation
- material-preparation guidance
- maintenance/care guidance
- a `peddigrohr vs holzschliff` comparison block
- FAQ

## Failure Mode to Avoid

Do not approve an article just because it:

- uses the target keyword
- follows the brief title
- includes product links

If it does not satisfy the real search intent and the audit-driven content gap, it is not good enough.
