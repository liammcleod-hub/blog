## The Emotional Register

Bastelschachtel operates in one primary emotional register:
**Warm recognition.**

She reads it and thinks: "Yes. That's exactly how I feel."

Not impressed. Not convinced. Not excited by an offer. Recognized.

The brand's job is to reflect her back to herself — her desire to make things, her creative identity she's let go quiet, the pleasure of handing someone something she made with her hands. When she feels seen, everything else follows.

---

## Tone Dimensions

### Warmth — HIGH
Always. Even in functional emails (shipping updates, stock notices). The warmth is structural, not performative. It comes from writing as if one real person is writing to one real person.

### Urgency — NEVER MANUFACTURED
Real deadlines (shipping cutoffs, genuinely limited stock) may be stated as fact. They are never amplified with emotional pressure. "Die letzte GLS-Abholung ist am 19. Dezember um 10 Uhr." Full stop. No "hurry," no "last chance," no countdown language.

### Excitement — GENUINE ONLY
Excitement is allowed when it's real. A new product that arrived this morning. A technique just discovered. A customer photo that made the team stop what they were doing. Manufactured excitement for promotional purposes reads as noise and breaks trust.

### Authority — LIGHT
Bastelschachtel is a guide, not an expert. The tone is "we found this and love it" not "as experts in the field." Small shop energy: humble, knowledgeable, approachable.

### Intimacy — CALIBRATED
Close but not over-familiar. "Du" throughout. But not assuming deep personal knowledge she hasn't shared. Think: a warm acquaintance who understands your creative interests, not a best friend reading your diary.

## Tone by Seasonal Context

### January–August (Off-Season)
Reflective. Community-minded. Patient. Low energy, not in a bad way — in a January morning way. Stories, inspiration, maker reflections. No pressure to buy anything.

### September–October (Approaching Season)
Anticipatory. The excitement of something building. Projects forming in the mind. Materials arriving. The season starting to feel real.

### November–December (Peak Season)
Warm urgency — but urgency from the *meaning* of the moment, never from artificial scarcity. "Weihnachten kommt" is not a threat, it's an invitation. The tone is a friend saying "es ist Zeit."

## What Tone Changes Between Contexts

| Context | Changes | Stays the Same |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Sale email | More direct, slightly concise | Warm, no pressure language |
| New product | More excited, sensory | Still personal, still "du" |
| Customer story | More quiet, moved | Same warmth and intimacy |
| Monthly newsletter | Full prose poetry mode | Same voice |
| Shipping deadline | Factual, calm | No panic language |

## Tone Failure Modes to Flag

When AI-generated email content displays these patterns, flag as **tone violation**:

1. **Pressure inflation** — Adding urgency or scarcity to non-urgent situations
2. **Corporate slide** — "Wir freuen uns, Ihnen mitteilen zu dürfen..." or similar formal constructions
3. **Gratitude overload** — "Danke, dass du Teil unserer Community bist" (performative, not genuine)
4. **False exclusivity** — "Nur für unsere engste Community" (manufactured VIP feeling)
5. **Motivational poster** — Generic creative-empowerment language without specificity
6. **Feature listing** — Describing products by specs rather than by what they make possible
7. **Reflexive apology** — Apologizing for emailing, apologizing for selling (breaks trust, implies shame)
8. **Business interiority** — Mentioning revenue goals, email strategy, or marketing intent in customer-facing copy
