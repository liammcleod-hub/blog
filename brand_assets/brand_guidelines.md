## What Bastelschachtel Is

A craft supply business that sells creativity, permission, and the experience of making — not just products. The hero offering is build-your-own DIY bundles ("Schachteln") across 3,000+ individual SKUs. Every order is hand-packed in Wattens.

The brand exists at the intersection of:
- **Creative possibility** — What she can make
- **Permission** — That it's okay to take the time
- **Ease** — The system makes it simple to start

---

## Who It Serves

**Primary:** Women 30–60 in the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland).

**Customer archetype: The Suppressed Creative**
- Used to create for herself; now creates primarily for others
- Carries guilt about taking creative time for herself
- Wants to give meaningful, handmade gifts but feels overwhelmed or unskilled
- Responds to permission, not pressure
- The appreciation from recipients outweighs the friction — when framed correctly

**She is not:** A hobbyist looking for the cheapest price. A beginner who needs convincing. A passive consumer. She already wants to create. She needs the door opened and the path cleared.

---

## Brand Positioning

**Old (wrong):** "We help overwhelmed people make thoughtful gifts by removing decision fatigue."
**Correct:** "Bastelschachtel is for people who already want to make gifts but didn't know where to start. We're the permission and the system."

This distinction is critical. The brand does not create desire — it releases desire that already exists. Every piece of copy, every email, every ad should assume she wants to create and position Bastelschachtel as removing the barrier, not installing the motivation.

---

## Brand Values (In Priority Order)

1. **Warmth** — Every touchpoint feels human and personal
2. **Authenticity** — Do what you say, say what you do. Small team, honest mistakes, direct phone line.
3. **Permission over pressure** — Invite. Never demand. Let her come at her own pace.
4. **Quality over scale** — 800 engaged readers > 3,000 reluctant ones
5. **Community first** — Long-term relationship > single transaction
6. **Honest imperfection** — "We're small. We make mistakes sometimes. Call us and we fix it immediately."

---

## Brand Promise

> "We make your order ourselves, by hand, every day. We're small, we make mistakes sometimes, and we're happy when you call us directly if something's not right. Then we fix it immediately."

This promise appears in every email footer. It is a differentiator, not a disclaimer.

---

## Email Program Rules

- **Frequency:** 1–2x/month off-season; up to 3–4x/week during peak (Oct–Dec)
- **Send times:** Non-round numbers (e.g., 10:30, 15:30) — feels personal, not scheduled
- **Language:** German, always "du" form. English versions exist for some content but are written fresh, not translated.
- **Structure:** Prose poetry. Short lines. White space. No bullet lists in the letter body.
- **CTA style:** Soft invitation. "Schau mal." "Hier entdecken." Never "JETZT KAUFEN."
- **Sender:** "Bastelschachtel" or "das Bastelschachtel-Team"
- **Footer:** Always includes hand-packing promise + direct phone/contact invitation

## Product Architecture

Five bundle categories (permanent):
1. **Für Weihnachten** — Holiday gift-making
2. **Für dich selbst** — Self-directed projects (permission angle)
3. **Zum Verschenken** — Gift-giving for makers
4. **Für zu Hause** — Home decoration
5. **Mit Kindern** — Family crafting

Top product families: Glasätzungspaste, Korbflechten, Metallic/Wachspasten, Krepppapier, Rocailles, Peddigrohr.

---

## Strategic Priority

**Primary goal:** Reduce Amazon dependency (currently ~90% of revenue). Grow owned channel (email + Shopify) toward 30–40% of revenue. Email is the primary owned-channel lever.

**What this means for emails:** Each email builds the relationship and the channel, not just drives a single transaction. Brand equity is the product. Trust is the strategy.

---

## What the Brand Is NOT

- ❌ Corporate or scaled-feeling
- ❌ Aggressive or urgency-driven
- ❌ Transaction-focused
- ❌ Generic (could be any craft shop)
- ❌ Fake-exclusive ("VIP members only")
- ❌ Self-conscious about being a business ("This isn't a sales email...")
- ❌ Apologetic about selling

---

## Localization

Wattens and Tirol appear in the voice as authentic origin — never as a marketing gimmick. "Grüße aus Wattens" because that is where the team actually is. Not as a quality signal or regional branding strategy.
