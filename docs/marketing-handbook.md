# PART 1: BRAND IMMERSION MANIFESTO

---

## SECTION 1: THE PHILOSOPHY

### What Bastelschachtel Stands For

**The Core Belief:**

> She already wants to create. She always has. Somewhere between raising kids, running a household, working, and being everything to everyone, she stopped. Not because she lost the desire — because life made it feel impossible.

Bastelschachtel exists to remove the barriers between her and the thing she's been wanting to make.

**Not a craft shop. A permission slip.**

---

### The "Why" (What Competitors Don't Have)

| Competitor | What They Do | What Bastelschachtel Does Instead |
|------------|--------------|----------------------------------|
| **Amazon** | Sells products at scale. Impersonal. Transactional. | Recognizes her. Knows she's making something meaningful. |
| **Boesner / VBS** | Broadcasts "buy more stuff." Broad assortment, no direction. | Curates. Reduces decision fatigue, not just product count. |
| **Generic craft shops** | Lists features. Describes materials by specs. | Describes outcomes. What the thing makes possible, not what's in the box. |
| **Pinterest / YouTube** | Inspires. Creates desire. Then leaves her stuck. | Completes the circle. Materials + permission + path = done. |
| **Fixed DIY kits** | Decides everything for her. Restrictive. | Gives structure without removing choice. |

---

### The Five Brand Pillars

1. **Warmth** — Every word is written by a person who cares, for a person who cares.
2. **Authenticity** — Four people in Wattens. Hand-packed every day. Direct phone line.
3. **Permission over Pressure** — She doesn't need to be convinced. She needs to be invited.
4. **Quality over Scale** — 800 people who read every email > 8,000 people who ignore it.
5. **Honest Imperfection** — "We're small. We make mistakes. Call us and we fix it."

---

### The Tagline

> *"Hand-gepackt. Erreichbar. Immer."*

Not "Best Quality!" Not "Lowest Prices!" Not "Huge Selection!"

Just three truths that competitors can't credibly claim:
- **Hand-packed** — Someone who cares touched every package.
- **Erreichbar** — You can reach us. Directly. A real human answers.
- **Immer** — Always. Not just during business hours. Not just when it's convenient. Always.

---

### The Brand Promise (Sacrosanct)

> "We make your order ourselves, by hand, every day. We're small, we make mistakes sometimes, and we're happy when you call us directly if something's not right. Then we fix it immediately."

This is not a disclaimer. This is the differentiator.

## SECTION 2: THE VOICE CONSTRAINTS

### The Golden Rule

**Before any sentence:** Does this sound like a letter from a creative friend — or like a marketing campaign?

If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it.

---

### NEVER (Hard Stops)

#### Category 1: Never Create Pressure

❌ "Letzte Chance"
❌ "Nur noch heute"
❌ "Verpasse nicht"
❌ "Jetzt sofort"
❌ "Schnell zugreifen"
❌ "Fast ausverkauft" *(unless it is literally true)*
❌ Countdown timers of any kind

**Exception:** Real factual deadlines are fine. "Die letzte GLS-Abholung ist am 19. Dezember um 10 Uhr." — Calm. Factual. No amplification.

---

#### Category 2: Never Treat Her Like a Conversion Target

❌ "Jetzt kaufen"
❌ "JETZT KAUFEN" *(or any ALL CAPS CTA)*
❌ "Klicke jetzt"
❌ "In den Warenkorb"
❌ "Sofort kaufen"

**Instead:** "Schau mal." "Hier entdecken." "Nimm dir Zeit und stöbere." "Wenn du magst."

---

#### Category 3: Never Fake Closeness

❌ "Nur für unsere engste Community"
❌ "Exklusiv für dich"
❌ "Du wurdest persönlich ausgewählt"
❌ "Als Erstes erfahren"
❌ "Insider-Zugang"

**Instead:** "Für dich." Direct. No performance.

---

#### Category 4: Never Expose the Machine

❌ "Q4"
❌ "Umsatz"
❌ "Conversion"
❌ "Engagement"
❌ "Diese E-Mail ist kein Verkaufs-Push"
❌ "Laut unseren Daten"

The customer should never see inside the business. She wants to feel like she's talking to a friend, not reading a marketing report.

---

#### Category 5: Never Use Corporate Language

❌ "Wir freuen uns, Ihnen mitteilen zu dürfen..."
❌ "Hiermit möchten wir Sie informieren..."
❌ "Mit freundlichen Grüßen"
❌ **"Sie" form** — Always use "du"

**Instead:** "Herzliche Grüße aus Tirol." Warm, informal, personal.

---

#### Category 6: Never Perform Warmth

❌ "Danke, dass du Teil unserer Community bist"
❌ "Wir sind so froh, dich zu haben"
❌ "Du bedeutest uns so viel"
❌ "Wir lieben unsere Kunden"
❌ "Ohne dich wäre das alles nicht möglich"

Real warmth shows up in *how* you write, not in *what you claim* to feel.

---

### ALWAYS (Rules to Live By)

✅ **Use "du" always.** Every person. Every sentence.

✅ **Write like one person to one person.** Not a broadcast.

✅ **Lead with creative possibility.** Product comes second.

✅ **Short sentences. Short paragraphs. White space is part of the voice.**

✅ **Line breaks signal pause, not list items.** No bullets in the letter body.

✅ **Soft CTAs only.** Invitation, not demand.

✅ **Seasons are real, not marketing hooks.** Write about autumn because autumn is happening, not because it's Q4.

✅ **Specificity over generic.** "Rocailles in 60 Farben" not "lots of colors."

✅ **Write from inside her creative life.** Never from inside business goals.

✅ **Genuine excitement only.** If you didn't actually feel it while writing, cut it.

---

### The Tone Spectrum

```
Too cold ←——————————————————→ Too warm
Corporate newsletter    [TARGET]    Over-familiar DMs

Too timid ←——————————————————→ Too pushy  
No product mention      [TARGET]    "JETZT KAUFEN"

Too generic ←————————————————→ Too niche
Could be any shop       [TARGET]    Only for Wattens locals
```

**Target:** Personal, specific, warm, unhurried, product-present-but-not-foregrounded.

---

### The Voice Check (Run Before Every Send)

- [ ] Does this sound like a letter from a creative friend?
- [ ] Would she feel invited or pressured?
- [ ] Is there any manufactured urgency?
- [ ] Does it give permission rather than create guilt?
- [ ] Is it in warm, personal "du" form?
- [ ] Is the product secondary to the creative possibility?
- [ ] Would she want to sit down and read this?
- [ ] Does this announce warmth or show it?

## SECTION 3: THE CUSTOMER PERSONA

### Her Emotional State When She Finds Us

**She is tired.** Not tired of crafting — tired of everything else that fills the hours before she gets to do what she actually wants.

**She is guilty.** Guilt about taking time for herself. About spending money on "just" art supplies. About starting something she might not finish.

**She is capable.** She has always made things. She remembers the pleasure of finishing something with her hands. She knows what a meaningful gift looks like.

**She is overwhelmed.** Too many choices. Too many decisions. She wanted to start in October. It's December. The Pinterest board exists. The project never began.

**She is ready.** Not to be taught. Not to be convinced. Just to be shown a path that doesn't require five more hours of research.

---

### Meet Her: The Suppressed Creative

**Age:** 30–60
**Location:** DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)
**Gender:** Primarily women

**What she wants:**
- To make something with her hands that means something
- To give a gift that feels personal, not transactional
- To reclaim the creative identity she let go quiet
- To feel like the person who *does* this, not the person who *means to*

**What stops her:**
- "I don't have time"
- "I'm not good enough"
- "I don't know where to start"
- "It's too complicated"
- Guilt

**What she needs:**
- Permission. Not more information.
- A clear path, not a blank page.
- Someone who takes her seriously.

---

### What She's Saying (Real VOC)

**When she's happy:**
> "Glasätzpaste funktioniert wunderbar"
> "Ich benutze nur noch Produkte von Pentart."
> "Das Reispapier kam sicher verpackt und schnell bei mir an."
> "sehr detailreiche Siliconform"
> "Riesige Auswahl"
> "da kommt ein Stück Kindheit zurück"

**What she's really saying:**
- "I trust this to actually work."
- "I found someone who understands my craft."
- "This feels like it was picked for someone like me."
- "The materials arrived with care."

---

### What She Fears (Objections)

| Fear | Response |
|------|----------|
| "I'm not creative enough" | You already know what a meaningful gift looks like. We make it easier to start with the right materials. |
| "This looks complicated" | We remove sourcing friction. The path is clearer, not more decisions. |
| "I don't have time" | The promise is not elaborate artistry. It's a more meaningful result with a simpler path. |
| "Amazon is easier" | Amazon is easier for transactions. We are better when the outcome needs to feel personal. |
| "What if I buy the wrong things?" | We're a guide, not just a catalog. Call us. We help you figure it out. |

---

### Her Three Jobs To Be Done

1. **Help me make a meaningful gift** that feels personal, not generic.
2. **Help me start a creative project** without having to figure out every material myself.
3. **Help me reclaim creative time** for myself without guilt or friction.

---

### What She Cares About (In Priority Order)

1. That it works. (She doesn't want to fail. She wants confidence.)
2. That it's easy to start. (Not a new skill, just a new project.)
3. That it feels personal. (Made by her, not bought.)
4. That the materials are quality. (She knows the difference.)
5. That someone who understands her chose these things. (Curation, not volume.)

---

### What She Always Needs to Hear

✅ "Du weißt schon, was du machen willst."
*(You already know what you want to make.)*

✅ "Wir helfen dir, anzufangen."
*(We help you get started.)*

✅ "Schau mal, was möglich ist."
*(Look, what's possible.)*

✅ "Nimm dir Zeit."
*(Take your time.)*

✅ "Das Paket kommt von uns. Hand-gepackt."
*(The package comes from us. Hand-packed.)*

## 1. THE FRAU POPP STANDARD

### What Happened

A customer named Romy Popp received a wrong package. Here's how Bastelschachtel handled it:

**First response:**
- Thanked her explicitly for her "großes Verständnis" (great understanding)
- Let her KEEP the wrong items (Korbböden) — no return requested
- "Wir kümmern uns um den Rest" (We take care of everything else)
- Set a clear follow-up commitment: "Ich werde mich morgen wieder bei Ihnen melden"

**Second response (delayed due to Easter rush):**
- Acknowledged the delay without excuses: *"Im Ostergeschäft ist uns hier leider ein wenig die Zeit zwischen den Fingern zerronnen"*
- Packed her entire original order FRESH — didn't just refund or resend
- Added a small surprise gift for her children's creative space
- Called her patience *"Engelsgeduld"* (angelic patience) — elevated her response instead of minimizing their failure

---

### What This Teaches Us

| Traditional Business Logic | Bastelschachtel Logic |
|---------------------------|----------------------|
| Mistake = cost. Minimize it. | Mistake = opportunity. Invest in the relationship. |
| "Keep the wrong item, here's a 10% refund." | "Keep the wrong item, here's your real order, and a surprise for your kids." |
| Delay = liability. Apologize minimally. | Delay = honesty. Explain naturally, then over-deliver. |
| Customer upset = problem to solve. | Customer upset = human to recognize. |

**The core philosophy:**
> Mistakes are not problems to minimize. They are moments to create a customer for life.

When something goes wrong, the question is never "how do we limit our exposure?" The question is always "how do we turn this into a story she'll tell?"

---

### The Test for Any Customer Service Response

1. Will this email make her feel *recognized* or *processed*?
2. Are we investing in the relationship or closing a ticket?
3. Would she tell this story to a friend?

## 2. THE CREATIVE PARTNER PERSONA

### The Shift: From "Shop" to "Peer"

When a customer shares their work, the relationship changes:

**Example 1: The Tablet (customer project photo)**

> "Wow ist das cool geworden!! Das sieht super aus! Damit nimmst du für März am Gewinnspiel teil — und ich glaube deine Chancen stehen hoch!!"

- They lead with genuine enthusiasm: "Wow ist das cool geworden!!"
- They position the customer as a CREATOR, not a buyer
- They show personal investment — speculating as a fellow creative, not as a system

**Note:** Multiple exclamation marks are normally forbidden in broadcast emails — but in personal responses to customer creativity, genuine excitement breaks the rules.

**Example 2: Customer craft photos (general)**

> "Vielen, vielen Dank für die wunderbaren Bastelfotos im Anhang, wir haben uns riesig gefreut! Es ist immer eine große Inspiration für uns zu sehen, was unsere Kunden alles Kreatives erschaffen."

- They flip the relationship. Customer creativity INSPIRES THEM.
- "Inspiration für uns" — positions the brand as a learner, not a teacher
- The customer is the expert. They curate and support. They don't lecture.

---

### The Creative Partner Rule

| Before sharing | After sharing |
|----------------|---------------|
| Customer is the buyer | Customer is a creative peer |
| We guide | We learn from |
| We have expertise | We have appreciation |

**Never reply to customer creativity with:**
❌ "Vielen Dank für Ihre Nachricht."
❌ "Wir freuen uns über Ihr Feedback."
❌ "Ihr Foto wurde erfolgreich eingereicht."

## 3. THE SMALL BUSINESS SHIELD

### How "Smallness" Becomes Trust

From the abandoned cart emails:

> "Kundenzufriedenheit ist uns als kleines Familienunternehmen sehr wichtig."

**Shield Type 1: Permission to care**
> "als kleines Familienunternehmen" = "we notice every cart because we notice every customer"

**Shield Type 2: The discount as care, not desperation**

> "Deshalb möchte ich dir gerne einen kleinen Rabatt von 10% mit dem Code Jetzt10 anbieten."

The discount is framed as the natural response of someone who noticed — not a desperate conversion tactic.

**Shield Type 3: Direct access as proof**

- "rufen Sie uns an" — direct phone number, not a ticket system
- "melde dich einfach" — informal, personal, reachable
- "wir helfen dir gerne weiter" — no queue, no bot, no form
- Sign-offs like "Bernhard" or "Liam" — real names, not "Customer Service Team"

---

### The Smallness Template

When you need to explain who Bastelschachtel is:

```
Wir sind ein kleines Familienunternehmen in Wattens, Tirol.
Jede Bestellung wird bei uns per Hand gepackt.
Wenn etwas schiefgeht, erreichst du uns direkt.
```

Never:
❌ "Als mittelständischer Betrieb..."
❌ "Unser Kundenservice-Team..."
❌ "Gemäß unseren Unternehmensrichtlinien..."

## 4. LINGUISTIC "NO-GO" ZONES

### Corporate / Stiff German Phrases They Avoid

| ❌ Never | Why | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|-----|----------------|
| "Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren" | Bureaucratic. Distant. | "Liebe [KUNDE]" or "Hallo [KUNDE]" |
| "Wir freuen uns, Ihnen mitteilen zu dürfen" | Corporate. Formal. | "Wir haben gute Neuigkeiten" |
| "Hiermit möchten wir Sie informieren" | Notice-board language | "Ich wollte dir kurz sagen..." |
| "Gemäß unserer Richtlinien" | Process-over-people | "Das machen wir gerne so" |
| "Wir bitten um Ihr Verständnis" | Defensive. Apologetic. | "Danke für dein Verständnis!" — or just deliver |
| "Selbstverständlich" | Formal filler | "Klar!" or "Natürlich gerne!" |
| "Vielen Dank im Voraus" | Assumes compliance | "Danke, wenn du das machen könntest" |
| "Anbei erhalten Sie" | Email template default | "Ich hab dir das mal angehängt" |
| "Für weitere Fragen stehen wir Ihnen zur Verfügung" | Corporate CS language | "Melde dich einfach, wenn was ist" |
| "Mit freundlichen Grüßen" | Stiff closing | "Liebe Grüße" / "Herzliche Grüße aus Tirol" |

---

### The Real Test

Before any sentence, ask:

> "Would Bernhard write this?"

If it sounds like it came from a German government office, a bank, or a call center training manual — rewrite it.

## 5. SAMPLE WELCOME EMAIL

### 3-Sentence Welcome Email for a New Customer

> **Hallo [Name],**
>
> schön, dass du da bist. Wir sind die Bastelschachtel — ein kleines Familienunternehmen aus Wattens, Tirol. Jede Bestellung wird bei uns von Hand gepackt, und wenn irgendetwas nicht passt, erreichst du uns direkt und wir lösen das sofort.
>
> Viel Freude beim Stöbern. Und wenn du Fragen hast — meld dich jederzeit.
>
> Herzliche Grüße aus Tirol,
> **[Name]**

---

### Why It Works

- **"schön, dass du da bist"** — Welcoming, personal, warm. Not "Willkommen in unserem Onlineshop."
- **"kleines Familienunternehmen aus Wattens"** — Immediately establishes authenticity and origin.
- **"von Hand gepackt"** — The differentiator, stated simply.
- **"erreichst du uns direkt"** — The open door. Not a contact form, not a ticket number.
- **"wenn irgendetwas nicht passt"** — No performance of perfection. Acknowledges that things happen.
- **"Viel Freude beim Stöbern"** — Invitation, not demand. "Stöbern" (browse around) is the right verb — unhurried, curious.
- **"Melde dich jederzeit"** — Direct, personal, no friction.
- **"Herzliche Grüße aus Tirol"** — The signature closing. Not "Mit freundlichen Grüßen."

---

### The Test

Would this email make a new customer think:
*"Oh — this is different. These are real people."*

If yes, it's Bastelschachtel.
