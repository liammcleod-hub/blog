**Why 3, not 2 or 4:**

•1 email is too short; doesn't give permission time to sink in

•4 emails feels like pressure (especially with the full list)

•3 gives you: **permission + inspiration + practical deadline**

**Timeline:**

•**Email 1 (today, April 18):** Atmospheric permission-setting. "It's time to think about this."

•**Email 2 (May 1–3):** The moment she decides. Products + ideas + permission hybrid.

•**Email 3 (May 6–7):** Shipping deadline. Factual, calm.

Same structure repeats for Father's Day in June.

Gender Segmentation: Yes, But Thoughtfully

**Here's my recommendation:**

Send **different subject lines + opening paragraphs** based on gender data, but the **body + products stay the same**.

Why:

•A woman buying for her mother has different emotional context than a man buying for his mother

•A man who makes things is still part of your community — but his relationship to "Mother's Day gifting" is different

•Same products, different permission angle
