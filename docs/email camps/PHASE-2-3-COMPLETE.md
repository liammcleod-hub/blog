#### Part 1: Conversational Rhythm
- Natural speech patterns vs composed prose
- Slight redundancy for emphasis
- Casual conjunctions
- Natural "auch" placement
- Comma removal for flow

#### Part 2: Emphasis Density
- One moment per thought rule
- When to use wirklich/eigentlich/überhaupt
- Natural stacking vs over-stacking
- Examples of good and bad density

#### Part 3: Warm Colloquialisms
- "überhaupt kein Problem" vs "vollkommen in Ordnung"
- "bei uns direkt" vs "hier"
- "oder was mit Reispapier" vs "oder etwas mit"
- "reicht für so 8-10 Figuren" (casual "so")

#### Part 4: Permission Through Tone
- Show don't state permission
- Immediate alternatives instead of explicit statements
- Natural flow vs announcing permission

#### Part 5: Don't Be Prescriptive
- Never "genau richtig" or "perfekt"
- Invitation over instruction
- Examples of non-prescriptive language

#### Part 6: Lead with Positive
- Avoid "not" framing
- Focus on what IS true
- Examples of positive vs negative framing

#### Part 7: Philosophical Asides
- Casual profundity with "eigentlich"
- Brief, not staged
- Examples of good vs precious

#### Part 8: Humble, Not Performing
- Don't announce warmth
- Just be warm
- Examples of performing vs real warmth

#### Part 9: Separate Beats
- Let thoughts breathe
- Not too compressed, not too choppy
- Balance examples

#### Part 10: Location Warmth
- "bei uns direkt" / "hier in Wattens"
- Real place presence
- Avoid generic corporate language
