## Brand Voice Compliance

All emails adhere to Bastelschachtel brand voice guidelines:

### ✅ Voice Principles Applied

1. **Warm, personal "du" form** — throughout all emails
2. **Prose poetry structure** — short lines, intentional breaks, rhythm
3. **Permission over pressure** — explicit "das ist in Ordnung" statements
4. **Implicit selling** — products inside stories, not as headlines
5. **Seasonal grounding** — real moments, not marketing hooks
6. **Customer-centric always** — no business interiority language

### ✅ Forbidden Phrases Avoided

- ❌ No "Jetzt kaufen" / "Letzte Chance" / "Verpasse nicht"
- ❌ No false urgency or countdown timers
- ❌ No fake exclusivity ("Nur für VIP-Mitglieder")
- ❌ No business interiority ("Q4", "Umsatz", "Conversion")
- ❌ No corporate register (always "du" not "Sie")
- ❌ No performative warmth ("Danke, dass du Teil unserer Community bist")

### ✅ Soft CTAs Used

| Instead of | We Use |
|------------|--------|
| "Jetzt kaufen" | "Schau mal" / "Schau dich um" |
| "In den Warenkorb" | "Hier entdecken" |
| "Jetzt bestellen" | "Nimm dir Zeit" |
| "Klicke jetzt" | "Wenn du magst, findest du alles hier" |

## Campaign Strategy Highlights

### Mother's Day Unique Angles

**Female Audience:**
- Self-gifting permission built in ("weil du dir auch etwas schuldest")
- Mother-daughter making angle
- Post-Mother's Day self-care seed planted in Email 3

**Male Audience:**
- Removes craft identity assumption
- Emphasizes "doesn't have to be perfect"
- Strong phone support for guidance
- Kid collaboration as helper role

### Father's Day Unique Angles

**Female Audience:**
- Acknowledges Father's Day gets less emotional energy
- Masculine gift framing (practical, solid, not sentimental)
- Kid collaboration to make gift more meaningful

**Male Audience:**
- Father-son making as primary angle (not gift-buying)
- Memory/legacy framing ("Sie werden sich daran erinnern")
- Learning angle ("Sie lernen, wie man etwas Richtiges macht")
- Alternative: gift for his own father (secondary)

## Key Differentiators from Generic Holiday Emails

### ❌ What We DON'T Do

- No countdown timers or manufactured scarcity
- No "limited time offers" (unless factually true)
- No generic "show your appreciation" platitudes
- No product-first messaging
- No aggressive CTAs
- No guilt-tripping ("Don't forget!")

### ✅ What We DO

- **Permission narrative** — "It's okay to take time for this"
- **Emotional truth** — Honest about holiday dynamics
- **Alternative outcomes** — "Time together" as valid gift
- **Explicit anti-pressure** — "Das ist keine Drohung. Nur eine Information."
- **Self-gifting angle** — Especially post-Mother's Day
- **Phone support** — Personal connection, removes intimidation
- **Prose poetry** — Breathing room, not walls of text

## Campaign Philosophy

This campaign is built on Bastelschachtel's core positioning:

Mother's Day and Father's Day are not sales deadlines.

Not push. Not pressure. Not guilt.
