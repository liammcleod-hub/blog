## 1. Voice Profile Card

### The Four Dimensions

| Dimension | Score | What This Means for Bastelschachtel |
|-----------|-------|-------------------------------------|
| **Funny ← Serious** | **3 / 5** | Warm and grounded. Occasionally wry. Never slapstick. Never stiff. The kind of funny that shows up in how they handle mistakes ("Engelsgeduld" — angelic patience) rather than in jokes. |
| **Formal ← Casual** | **5 / 5** | Always "du." Always informal. Prose poetry. Short lines. White space. The customer is being written to by a person who knows her name, not a department. |
| **Respectful ← Irreverent** | **2 / 5** | Warm, patient, forgiving. Never snarky. Never preachy. But not stiff about it. They give customers the benefit of the doubt. Mistakes are "opportunities to create a customer for life." |
| **Enthusiastic ← Matter-of-fact** | **4 / 5** | Genuine excitement only. If it wasn't real while writing, cut it. Let the product and the craft speak. The brand is calm and confident — not hyping, not performing. |

### Voice Profile Summary

> **Bastelschachtel: Serious-leaning (3), Extremely Casual (5), Gently Respectful (2), Genuinely Restrained (4)**

**Translation:** A warm, unhurried, informal voice that respects the customer's creative identity and never performs enthusiasm it doesn't feel. Think: the friend who writes you a letter about a craft project she just discovered — not a marketing department trying to convert you.

## 3. Vocabulary Guide

### Words We Use

| Word / Phrase | Why It Works |
|---------------|--------------|
| **hand-gepackt** | The differentiator in two syllables. Real. Undeniable. |
| **erreichbar** | Reachable. Direct. A real human, not a ticket system. |
| **immer** | Always. Not just when it's convenient. |
| **Schau mal** | The signature soft CTA. Invitation, not demand. |
| **Stöbern** | To browse — but with curiosity, not intent. The right verb. |
| **schön, dass du da bist** | Welcoming without being sentimental. Personal. |
| **für dich** | Direct. Not "für unsere Community." For you specifically. |
| **Wir kümmern uns** | We take care of it. The buck stops here. |
| **von Hand** | By hand. Emphasizes the human touch in every package. |
| **Engelsgeduld** | Angelic patience. Used when a customer shows unusual grace. See [[bastelschachtel-soul-first]] for real usage. |
| **die Zeit zwischen den Fingern zerronnen** | "Time slipped through our fingers." Natural explanation for delay, not an excuse. See [[bastelschachtel-soul-first]] for real usage. |
| **leicht zu verarbeiten** | Easy to work with. The customer's most repeated phrase. |
| **gute Deckkraft** | Good coverage. Outcome language, not product spec. |
| **funktioniert wunderbar** | Works wonderfully. Confidence-building. |
| **sehr detailreich** | Very detailed. Respects the customer's craft intelligence. |
| **Riesige Auswahl** | Huge selection. Discovery as a value proposition. |
| **Wattens, Tirol** | Where the team actually is. Authentic origin, not branding. |

### Jargon Policy

| Jargon Type | Policy |
|-------------|--------|
| **Craft-specific terms** (Pentart, Reispapier, Peddigrohr, Glasätzpaste) | Use naturally. Don't explain what she already knows. |
| **German craft terminology** | Use German. Don't translate craft terms. |
| **Technical product specs** | Lead with outcomes. "Deckkraft" before "Pigmentdichte." |
| **Business terms** | Internal only. Never in customer-facing copy. |

## 4. Channel Tone Guide

| Channel | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Email (Newsletter)** | Full prose poetry mode. Most literary. Most unhurried. | Line breaks as rhythm. No bullets. White space as voice. |
| **Email (Transactional)** | Warmer, more personal. Same voice, slightly more functional. | "Deine Bestellung ist unterwegs" not "Your order has shipped." |
| **Email (Customer Service)** | Most empathetic. Human over process. Mistakes = opportunities. | "Das tut mir leid. Wir lösen das sofort." + action. |
| **Email (Abandoned Cart)** | Small business shield. "Wir haben bemerkt" → gentle care, not desperation. | See [[bastelschachtel-soul-first]] for real examples. |
| **Social Media** | +1 casual from baseline. More reactive. Still warm. | Responds to craft photos with genuine enthusiasm. |
| **Product Pages** | Outcome-first. Specific. Lead with what it makes possible. | "Für Gläser, Spiegel und kreative Geschenke" not "Glasätzpaste 150ml." |
| **Category Pages** | Permission-first. Creative possibility before product listing. | "Was wirst du diesen Frühling erschaffen?" before navigation. |
| **Blog / Tutorial Content** | Guide voice. "Wir haben das ausprobiert und..." | Not "As experts, we recommend..." |

## 5. Before / After Examples

### Example 1: Abandoned Cart Email

**Before (Generic):**
> "Wir haben bemerkt, dass du deinen Warenkorb noch nicht abgeschlossen hast."

**After (Bastelschachtel):**
> "Hallo [Name],
>
> wir haben bemerkt, dass deine Bestellung noch nicht ganz fertig ist.
>
> Kein Problem. Manchmal braucht man ein bisschen Zeit.
>
> Falls du noch Fragen hast — wir sind jederzeit erreichbar.
>
> Herzliche Grüße aus Tirol,
> Bernhard"

See more abandoned cart examples in [[bastelschachtel-soul-first]].

### Example 2: New Product Announcement

**Before (Corporate):**
> "Wir freuen uns, Ihnen mitteilen zu dürfen, dass unser neues Sortiment... verfügbar ist."

**After (Bastelschachtel):**
> "Eine neue Lieferung ist eingetroffen.
>
> Pentart-Farben — metallic, vintage, strukturiert.
> Genauso, wie du sie vielleicht schon kennst.
>
> Wir haben sie gerade ausgepackt. Noch nicht mal sortiert.
>
> Schau mal, wenn du magst."

### Example 3: Customer Service Response (Wrong Package)

**Before (Defensive):**
> "Sehr geehrte Frau Popp, wir bitten um Entschuldigung für die fehlerhafte Lieferung."

**After (Bastelschachtel):**
> "Liebe Frau Popp,
>
> zuerst einmal möchte ich mich aufrichtig bei Ihnen entschuldigen.
>
> Fehler passieren leider, aber es ist toll, wenn man so konstruktiv damit umgehen kann wie Sie.
>
> Wir haben Ihre Bestellung gerade komplett neu gepackt — sie geht noch heute raus. Die Korbböden dürfen Sie behalten. Als kleine Wiedergutmachung haben wir eine Überraschung beigelegt.
>
> Vielen Dank für Ihre Engelsgeduld.
>
> Wir kümmern uns um den Rest."

## 6. Human Content Checklist

Before any piece of content, run these checks:

- [ ] **Human-Led Outline**: Does this have a clear narrative arc, not just product features?
- [ ] **The "Un-AI" Element**: Is there 1-3 things a real person would write that AI wouldn't generate? (e.g., "Engelsgeduld," "die Zeit zwischen den Fingern zerronnen," "Noch nicht mal sortiert.")
- [ ] **Radical Specificity**: Is every claim backed by something concrete? "Rocailles in 60 Farben" not "eine große Auswahl."
- [ ] **No Em-Dashes**: Use hyphens (-) or other punctuation. Never em-dashes (—) in content.
- [ ] **Avoid Verbal Clichés**: If this could have been written by Amazon or Boesner, rewrite it.
- [ ] **The Bernhard Test**: Would Bernhard write this? If it sounds like a German government office, a bank, or a call center, rewrite it.
- [ ] **Permission Check**: Does this invite, not demand? "Schau mal" not "Jetzt kaufen."
- [ ] **Warmth Check**: Does warmth show up in how you write, not in what you claim to feel?
- [ ] **Read Aloud**: Would you want to sit down and read this?

## 7. Quick Reference

### The Golden Rule

> "Does this sound like a letter from a creative friend — or like a marketing campaign?"

If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it.

### The Brand in One Line

> **Bastelschachtel is for people who already want to make things but didn't know where to start. We're the permission and the system.**

### The Tagline

> **Hand-gepackt. Erreichbar. Immer.**

### The Brand Promise

> "We make your order ourselves, by hand, every day. We're small, we make mistakes sometimes, and we're happy when you call us directly if something's not right. Then we fix it immediately."

### Signature Closings

- **Standard:** "Herzliche Grüße aus Tirol, / Dein Team von der Bastelschachtel"
- **Seasonal:** "Kreative Frühlingsgrüße aus Wattens, / Dein Bastelschachtel-Team"
- **Personal (rare):** "Mit kreativen Grüßen aus der Werkstatt, / [Name] und das Bastelschachtel-Team"

### Soft CTA Reference

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "Jetzt kaufen" | "Schau mal" |
| "Jetzt bestellen" | "Hier entdecken" |
| "In den Warenkorb" | "Nimm dir Zeit und stöbere" |
| "Klicke jetzt" | "Wenn du magst, findest du alles hier" |
| "Sofort kaufen" | "Schau dich um" |

### The Voice Check (Run Before Every Send)

- [ ] Does this sound like a letter from a creative friend?
- [ ] Would she feel invited or pressured?
- [ ] Is there any manufactured urgency?
- [ ] Does it give permission rather than create guilt?
- [ ] Is it in warm, personal "du" form?
- [ ] Is the product secondary to the creative possibility?
- [ ] Would she want to sit down and read this?
- [ ] Does warmth show up in how I write, or what I claim to feel?
