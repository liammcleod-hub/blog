# Blog SEO Pipeline SEO Inference Rules
#content-pipeline #blog

This document defines how the pipeline should infer required page structure beyond the explicit brief.

## Core Rule

The article should be judged against:

1. the research dossier
2. the Bastelschachtel SEO audit
3. the relevant SEO skill guidance

## Why This Matters

Briefs are useful but incomplete.

They often encode the chosen content format, but they do not always encode the full search-intent mix or the content-gap strategy the site needs.

## Required Inputs

When evaluating or revising, use:

- `docs/seo/bastelschachtel_seo_audit.docx`
- `repo-skills/marketing-library/skills/seo-audit/SKILL.md`
- `repo-skills/marketing-library/skills/programmatic-seo/SKILL.md`
- `repo-skills/marketing-library/skills/ai-seo/SKILL.md`

## What to Infer

Infer:

- dominant content types in the search results
- whether the page needs tutorial, comparison, FAQ, or care sections
- whether the page is likely too thin for the opportunity
- whether the structure is extractable enough for AI search

## Tutorial-Intent Rule

If the topic is strongly tutorial-driven, the article must contain genuine procedural value.

That means it should include:

- setup steps
- preparation guidance
- early technique instruction
- common beginner mistakes
- at least one real how-to or step-by-step block

Product recommendations may support the page, but they must not replace the instructional core.

## Example

For `peddigrohr`, a listicle alone is not enough if the search landscape is also rich in:

- how-to intent
- care intent
- material comparison intent

The page should likely include supporting blocks for those intents even if the brief did not fully spell them out.
